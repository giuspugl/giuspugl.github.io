def bin_gains(tau, fs,obs,  g) :

    gb = pl.zeros(nch)

1. Summary¶

10/20/2019

In this posting I show that we are able to track $\sim10$ percent gain fluctuations on the time scale of up to 1 minute by regressing the time samples (including noise and sky signal) with CMB Temperature template map. Moreover, smaller gain fluctuations (order of few per cents) are harder to track because noise affects the estimates, in this case a longer time scale for the gain estimation might be helpful. With respect to previous postings, the high pass filter adopted in this analysis is a Legendre polynomial filtering. To get rid of the higher order leakage coming from Monopole and Dipole due to gain drift we have to filter up to the 4th order. We apply this filtering on a chuncks of TODs related to the time scale $\tau$ we wanna perform the gain regression.

Simulation setups¶

Toast Params ( values from v0 Litebird-IMO wiki page:

  • Detector pairs: 2
  • frequency band: 89 GHz
  • T_obs : 5 hours
  • sampling freq: 20 Hz
  • precession period :192.348 min
  • spin period: 20 min
  • Noise simulated : white
  • NET : 30 uK sqrt(s)
  • HWP : False
  • spin angle : 50
  • precession angle :45
  • MADAM destriper (baseline=60 sec)
  • Input maps: Monopole, Dipole, CMB T , Galactic emission @90 GHz

Gain drift sim¶

Simulate per detector a time stream signal $g(t)$ fluctuating around 1 with a low frequency component with a 1/f dependence $fknee = 150 $ mHz .

Multiply $g$ by the detector TOD.

Gain estimation¶

The procedure to mitigate gain mismatches is performed on each chunck (related to $\tau$) of time samples $d_{t , t+\tau}$ of a given detector:

  1. Apply filter to $d_{t , t+\tau} $
  2. Regress $d_{t , t+\tau} $ against signal-only template $T$ and estimate the regression slope $\hat{g}$ as $$ \hat{g} = \frac { T^{t} d } { T^{t} T } $$
  3. Mitigate the leakage, dividing $d_{t , t+\tau} $ by $\hat{g}$

Fig2 (top) Pair sum TOD from 5 hours of LB sims. (solid blue) signal-only TOD without noise and gain drift, (solid orange) TOD encoding signal noise and gain drift, (for graphical purposes we remove the offset and scale it by 1%), (solid green) TOD with the mitigation has been applied every 10,5, and 1 minutes respectively going from left to right. (bottom) PSD of TODs shown in the top panels, we kept the same color scheme.

The peak at around 1 mHz is an effect of dipole modulation due to the spin period, (e.g. see solid blue and green TODs in the top panel). Notice that we observe in the post-mitigation PSD (green ) a reduction of power below $1 $ mHZ, implying that the drift has been mitigated. Moreover, one may notice the troughs in the PSD at frequencies exactly around $\tau^{-1}$. This is the expected effect due to the filtering time scale adopted to perform the gain estimates. At larger frequencies, the signal follows the input ones, noise starts to kick in at $>100 $ mHz.

Future Outlooks¶

  • The whole pipeline has been thoroughly tested and debugged and shows promising results when gain mitigation is performed every 5 minutes using CMB T as a regression template.

  • The signal is fully recovered at frequencies above the chosen mitigation time scale, i.e. $\ell \gtrsim 30$.

  • Next: perform 1 yr LB simulation and check the effects onto maps and power spectra.
